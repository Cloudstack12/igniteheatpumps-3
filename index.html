The Complete House Rewire Cost: A Comprehensive Guide


What is a Fresh Air Ventilation System?

The electrical system in a house is crucial for the safety and functionality of the home. It powers appliances, lighting, and other essential systems, making it an integral part of modern living. However, electrical systems can deteriorate over time, leading to potential hazards and decreased efficiency. If you notice flickering lights, frequently tripping breakers, or other electrical issues, it may be time for a complete house rewire. This blog aims to provide an overview of a complete house rewire cost, including factors affecting costs, average costs by house size and type, potential additional charges and upgrades, cost-saving tips, and considerations.

Understanding the House Rewiring Process

Explanation of what a complete house rewire entails

Rewiring a house involves replacing the electrical wiring system, including the cables, outlets, switches, and electrical panels. It aims to enhance safety, improve functionality, and meet electrical code requirements. The process typically involves removing the old wiring and installing new wiring throughout the house.


Evaluation of the existing electrical system

Before embarking on a complete house rewire, a thorough evaluation of the existing electrical system is necessary. This evaluation helps identify any outdated or damaged wiring, overloaded circuits, inadequate grounding, or other safety concerns that must be addressed during rewiring.


Planning and designing the new electrical layout

Once the evaluation is complete, the next step is to plan and design the new electrical layout. This involves determining the optimal placement of outlets, switches, and electrical panels to meet the electrical needs of each room. In addition, careful consideration is given to accommodate appliances, lighting fixtures, and other electrical devices.


Obtaining necessary permits and permissions

Before starting a complete house rewire, homeowners must obtain the necessary permits and permissions from local authorities or building departments. These permits ensure that the rewiring work complies with safety regulations and codes. Therefore, it is essential to consult with the relevant authorities and follow the required procedures to avoid legal or safety complications during rewiring.


Factors Affecting House Rewire Costs

Size and complexity of the house

The size and complexity of the house play a significant role in determining the cost of a complete house rewire. Larger houses require more wiring materials and labor hours to complete the rewiring process. In addition, complex house layouts with multiple floors or hard-to-reach areas may require additional effort and time from electricians, resulting in higher costs.


Type and age of the existing electrical system

The type and age of the existing electrical system affect the cost of a complete house rewire. For example, older systems may have outdated wiring that needs to be replaced entirely, which can increase the overall cost. Additionally, certain types of wiring, such as aluminum wiring, may require special considerations or replacements to meet current electrical codes, leading to higher expenses.


Accessibility of electrical wiring

The accessibility of the electrical wiring within the house can impact the cost of a complete rewire. If the wiring is easily accessible, such as in unfinished basements or accessible crawl spaces, the labor required for installation can be less time-consuming and costly. However, if the wiring is concealed within walls, ceilings, or inaccessible areas, additional effort and labor may be needed, driving up the overall cost.


Number and type of rooms

The number and type of rooms in a house also influence the rewiring costs. Houses with more rooms generally require more electrical outlets, switches, and wiring, increasing material and labor expenses. Additionally, specialized rooms such as kitchens or home offices may have specific electrical requirements, such as dedicated circuits for appliances or computer equipment, which can add to the overall cost.


Additional features and upgrades desired

Including additional features and upgrades during a complete house rewire can impact the overall cost. For example, if homeowners want to install smart home automation systems, outdoor lighting, or security systems as part of the rewiring project, these additional components will incur extra costs. In addition, upgrading to higher-capacity electrical panels or adding dedicated circuits for specific appliances or electrical devices will also contribute to the final price.


Considering these factors will help homeowners estimate the costs of a complete house rewire and plan their budget accordingly.


Cost Breakdown of a Complete House Rewire

Labor costs

  • Hiring licensed electricians

Hiring licensed electricians is crucial for a safe and effective house rewire. However, electricians' fees vary depending on their experience, qualifications, and location. Therefore, obtaining multiple quotes and selecting reputable professionals within the budget is essential.

  • Estimating labor hours:

Labor hours required for a complete house rewire depend on the size, complexity, and accessibility of the house. Electricians typically charge an hourly rate, so estimating the total labor hours helps determine the labor cost portion of the project.


Material costs

While it may seem counterintuitive, a well-designed fresh-air ventilation system can improve energy efficiency. By exchanging fresh air with minimal energy loss, the system reduces the need for excessive heating or cooling. Additionally, some ventilation systems, such as energy recovery ventilation (ERV), can recover heat or coolness from the outgoing air, further reducing energy consumption. This results in cost savings on energy bills while ensuring optimal indoor air quality.


Comfort and Well-being

  • Wiring materials

Wiring materials include cables, wires, junction boxes, and conduits needed for the rewiring process. The quantity and type of materials depend on the size of the house, electrical needs, and any specific requirements. In addition, the cost of wiring materials can vary based on quality and brand.

  • Electrical panels and outlets

Upgrading the electrical panel is often necessary during a house rewire. The cost includes the panel, circuit breakers, grounding components, and any additional wiring needed for installation. The number and type of outlets required throughout the house also contribute to the material costs.

  • Switches, dimmers, and fixtures:

The cost of switches, dimmers, and fixtures will depend on the desired quality, style, and quantity. Upgrading to energy-efficient or intelligent switches and dimmers may increase costs but can offer long-term benefits in terms of energy savings and convenience.


Permit fees and inspections

A house rewire must obtain permits and schedule inspections from local authorities. Permit fees vary depending on the location and the scope of the project. Therefore, it is crucial to factor in these fees when budgeting for the rewiring project.


Contingency budget for unexpected issues

Setting aside a contingency budget is prudent to account for any unexpected issues that may arise during the rewiring process. Unforeseen challenges, such as hidden wiring problems or structural issues, can require additional labor, materials, and time to resolve. A contingency budget helps ensure the project proceeds smoothly without financial strain.


Average Costs by House Size and Type

Small apartments or condominiums

Rewiring a small apartment or condominium generally costs less compared to larger houses. These units' compact size and simpler electrical layouts result in lower material and labor requirements. On average, rewiring a small apartment or condominium can range from $1,500 to $5,000, depending on accessibility, wiring complexity, and any additional features desired.

Single-family homes

Rewiring a single-family home is a more extensive undertaking compared to smaller units. The average cost for rewiring a single-family home can vary significantly depending on the electrical system's size, layout, and complexity. As a general guideline, the price for rewiring a single-family home can range from $5,000 to $15,000 or more. Factors such as the number of rooms, wiring accessibility, and any additional upgrades will influence the overall cost.


Large or multi-story houses

Large or multi-story houses typically have square footage and intricate electrical systems, producing higher rewiring costs. In addition, more rooms, multiple floors, and potentially complex layouts contribute to increased material and labor requirements. As a result, the average cost for rewiring a large or multi-story house can range from $15,000 to $30,000, depending on the specific factors involved. Therefore, it's essential to consider the size, complexity, and accessibility when budgeting for a rewiring project of this scale.


Potential Additional Costs and Upgrades

Upgrading to higher capacity electrical panels

Homeowners may upgrade their electrical panel to a higher capacity as part of a house rewiring project. This allows for increased electrical load and can accommodate modern appliances, technologies, and future expansions. However, upgrading to a higher-capacity electrical panel can incur additional costs, including the price of the panel, labor for installation, and potential adjustments to the wiring system.


Installation of dedicated circuits

Dedicated circuits are separate electrical circuits dedicated to specific appliances or equipment. They provide enhanced safety and prevent the overloading of circuits. During a rewiring project, homeowners may opt to install dedicated circuits for high-demand appliances like refrigerators, air conditioners, electric stoves, or home theater systems. The cost of installing dedicated circuits depends on the number of circuits needed and the complexity of the wiring required.


Wiring for smart home automation

Smart home automation systems offer convenience, energy efficiency, and enhanced control over various aspects of a home. Wiring for smart home automation involves integrating smart devices, such as lighting controls, thermostats, security, and entertainment systems, into the electrical system. The wiring cost for smart home automation depends on the complexity of the desired system and the number of devices to be integrated.


Adding outdoor lighting or security systems

Enhancing outdoor lighting or installing security systems can improve property safety, aesthetics, and security. However, the cost of adding outdoor lighting or security systems during a rewiring project will depend on factors such as the size of the outdoor area, the number of fixtures required, the type of security system chosen, and any additional features desired, such as motion sensors or surveillance cameras.


Cost-Saving Tips and Considerations

Obtaining multiple quotes from different electricians

To ensure you're getting a fair price for your complete house rewiring project, obtaining multiple quotes from different licensed electricians is recommended. This allows you to compare prices, evaluate the scope of work provided, and choose the most cost-effective option without compromising quality and expertise.


DIY vs. hiring a professional

While it may be tempting to consider a DIY approach to save on labor costs, rewiring a house is a complex and potentially dangerous task. Hiring a professional electrician for such projects is strongly advised to ensure safety and compliance with electrical codes. In addition, DIY mistakes can lead to costly repairs and rework or even pose safety hazards in the long run.


Timing the rewiring project strategically

Timing your rewiring project strategically can help save costs. For example, consider scheduling the project during slower seasons for electricians when their rates may be more competitive. Additionally, coordinating the project with other renovations or remodeling work can help reduce disruption and optimize efficiency, potentially saving time and money.


Exploring financing and payment options

Complete house rewiring projects can be a significant financial investment. Explore different financing options, such as home improvement loans or lines of credit, to help manage the upfront costs. Some electricians may also offer payment plans or financing options to make the expense more manageable. Be sure to evaluate these options and choose the one that best suits your financial situation.


In conclusion, a complete house rewire is a significant undertaking that requires careful planning and consideration. We have explored the various factors influencing the costs, including house size, accessibility, and additional upgrades. Budgeting labor, materials, permits, and potential contingencies are essential. However, the benefits of a safe and modern electrical system are well worth the investment. To ensure a successful project, it is highly recommended to consult with licensed electricians. They can provide personalized guidance, assess your needs, and offer professional expertise to ensure your house rewiring project is completed efficiently and to the highest standards.
